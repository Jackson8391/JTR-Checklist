Tent Setup 0 / 32 done
⚠
Big rule: No one should ever walk on the tent with shoes on. This causes holes. Ask pastoral leaders to reinforce this.
Phase 1 — Site prep & layout
1
Trailer & Measuring
Done
Park the trailer near the setup site (not on it) — keep it hitched for stability.
Use measuring tapes to check the fit and mark tent placement.
Confirm the church called 811 (or local equivalent) to verify no underground utilities.
2
Unloading
Done
Lay down tarps before unloading the tent.
Stack chairs and other equipment neatly to save space and stay organized.
3
Unrolling & Lacing the Tent
Done
Lay out all vinyl sections.
Pull from grommets or brackets only — never from the vinyl edge.
Evangelist laces tent sections from the center outward.
Secure ends tightly with extra knots.
4
Wrinkle Removal & Final Positioning
Done
Volunteers surround the tent and pull from all sides to eliminate wrinkles.
Make final tent placement adjustments — last chance before staking.
Phase 2 — Staking & raising
5
Stake Placement
Done
Use spray paint to mark each corner (2 stakes per corner).
Mark off every side grommet (~5 ft away from tent edge).
Use corner marks for accurate layout — not the tent itself.
6
Driving Stakes
Done
Leave 6 inches above ground for easy removal later.
Drive at a slight angle away from the tent.
Check strap type — some straps attach before driving the stake.
7
Raise the 4 Corner Poles First
Done
Corner poles are critical for stability. Do not release them until the tent is fully taken down.
Raise all 4 corner poles.
Confirm all 4 corners are stable before moving on.
8
Raise Initial Side Poles
Done
Only raise side poles as directed. Too many up at once will prevent center poles from being raised.
Raise initial side poles as directed by the Evangelist — do not get ahead.
Leave straps slightly loose — makes raising center poles easier.
9
Raise First Center Pole
Done
Push the pole through both metal grommets for stability.
Keep the pole as plumb (straight/vertical) as possible.
10
Repeat Side & Center Pole Pattern
Done
Continue pattern: raise side poles → raise center pole → repeat.
Continue until all center and side poles are up.
11
Back Wall & Final Adjustments
Done
Save back wall poles for last — raise them now.
Tighten and straighten each side pole.
Roll up excess straps as a visual check mark that the strap is complete.
Phase 3 — Final touches
12
Tie Internal Ropes
Done
Locate all internal ropes — these prevent the tent from lifting in high winds.
Pull each rope hard and tie securely to a pole.
13
Hang Lighting
Done
Confirm power source location (usually behind the stage).
Hang patio string lights around the perimeter edge.
Mount flood lights overhead.
First layer: weak tape to hold position.
Second layer: strong duct tape to secure.
Run all cords down the poles and under chairs.
14
Final Walkthrough
Done
Hold off on chairs, sound, and non-essentials until Friday morning to minimize security needs.
Re-inspect every strap and pole — re-level and re-tighten until perfect.
Set up baptism changing tents (if applicable).
Set up tables and signage.
Set up any additional accessories as directed.
Setup at a glance
Crew: 25 able-bodied volunteers (plus lighter-duty helpers)
Time: 3–4 hours with a good crew
Main setup: Tuesday evening — final items Friday morning
Follow the Evangelist team's lead at every stage
